The QuED (Quantum Entanglement Demonstrator) is a complete tabletop system that generates and measures polarization-entangled photon pairs, allowing students and researchers to directly observe quantum phenomena that were once only theoretical.

With QuED, users can perform fundamental quantum experiments including Bell's inequality tests, quantum state tomography, and entanglement visibility measurements - all in a compact, easy-to-use package.

Quantum Experiments Made Accessible

QuED brings cutting-edge quantum research to your lab or classroom

Entangled Photon Generation

At the heart of QuED is a Type-I spontaneous parametric down-conversion (SPDC) source that converts pump laser photons into entangled photon pairs. This process creates photons with perfectly correlated polarizations - a hallmark of quantum entanglement.

  • • Wavelength: 810 nm (degenerate)
  • • Source Type: Non-collinear Type-I SPDC
  • • Pair Rate: >10,000 pairs/second

Educational Insight:

The SPDC process demonstrates energy conservation (ħωpump = ħωsignal + ħωidler) and phase matching conditions fundamental to quantum optics.

Bell's Inequality Tests

QuED provides a complete setup for demonstrating violation of Bell's inequalities - the definitive experimental proof that quantum mechanics cannot be described by local hidden variable theories.

Typical Bell Parameter (S)

S > 2.3 (basic kit) / S > 2.4 (advanced)

Measurement Bases

Adjustable polarizer angles for CHSH inequality tests

The system includes all necessary optical components and alignment tools for students to perform these historic experiments themselves.
